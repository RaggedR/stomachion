# Stomachion Experiments

## Verification

The DLX solver finds **17,152 raw solutions** for the canonical Stomachion.

- 17,152 ÷ 8 (D4) = **2,144 geometrically distinct tilings** (distinct region-sets)
- 17,152 ÷ 32 = **536** (Cutler's count, which also mods out 2 congruent piece pairs B≡G, D≡E)

Solver: `solver.py` with array-based DLX at res=2 (24×24 grid).
Runtime: ~10 minutes in pure Python.

---

## Experiment 1: Coproduct exploration (beating 536)

**Question:** Can a 14-piece dissection beat the Stomachion's 536?

**Method:** Horizontal fault line y=6, splitting into two 12×6 halves with 7 pieces each.
Total solutions = product of half-solutions. 50 random trials.

**Result:** Trial 9 found 192 × 24 = **4,608**, beating 536 by ~9×.

**Caveat:** This searches only convex-piece dissections with forced fault lines.
The Stomachion has non-convex pieces and no forced fault lines — it's not in this search space.

## Observations

1. **Rigidity is generic.** Most random dissections have 1–2 geometric solutions.
   P(rigid) ≈ 1.3/n → 0 as n grows.

2. **The distribution is not Gaussian.** It is a sharp spike at low values with
   an exponentially decaying right tail. Concentrated at powers of 2.

3. **Powers of 2 dominate.** Counts of 1, 2, 4, 8, 16 account for >95% of all
   dissections at every n. This reflects the multiplicative coproduct structure:
   each independent exchange move doubles the count.

4. **The median transitions from 2 to 4** between n=7 and n=8 — the "typical"
   dissection gains its second independent exchange move around this point.

5. **Mean grows roughly linearly**: mean ≈ 0.9n.

6. **The max is stuck at 336** for n ≥ 7 — a recurring high-symmetry dissection
   that the random generator finds across different n values.

---

## Conjecture

The number of independent exchange moves k in a random n-piece lattice dissection
follows approximately **Poisson(λn)** for some small λ ≈ 0.15. The geometric tiling
count is approximately 2^k, giving a discrete log-normal-like distribution for the
count itself, with an exponentially heavy right tail.

The Stomachion (n=14, 2144 geometric tilings, log₂ ≈ 11) sits deep in the right tail —
not because it was optimized for flexibility, but because its "natural construction lines"
happen to create many edge-length coincidences on the integer lattice.

---

## Limitations

- **Convex pieces only.** The random cut generator produces only convex pieces.
  The Stomachion has non-convex pieces (pentagon, quadrilaterals). A fair comparison
  requires an arrangement-of-lines generator.

- **Random cuts ≠ random dissections.** The sequential-cut process samples a biased
  subset of all lattice dissections. Different generative processes might give
  different distributions.

- **Lattice only.** All vertices are constrained to the 12×12 integer grid.
  The full space of dissections (arbitrary vertex positions) is vastly larger,
  but almost all non-lattice dissections are rigid (1 solution).

## Experiment 3: All-irreducible dissections

**Question:** How common are dissections where *every* tiling is fault-free (no solution has an optional fault line)?

**Method:** For each n, scan 200 random dissections (100 for n≥14). For each, compute total and irreducible tiling counts via DLX. A dissection is "all-irreducible" if irred == total and total > 1.

### Results

| n | all-irred | valid | % | best all-irred count |
|---|-----------|-------|---|---------------------|
| 3 | 99 | 200 | 50% | 4 |
| 4 | 127 | 200 | 64% | 8 |
| 5 | 150 | 200 | 75% | 8 |
| 6 | 158 | 200 | 79% | 16 |
| 7 | 162 | 200 | 81% | 16 |
| 8 | 163 | 198 | 82% | 16 |
| 9 | 164 | 195 | 84% | 16 |
| 10 | 156 | 191 | 82% | 48 |
| 11 | 154 | 189 | 81% | 48 |
| 12 | 151 | 187 | 81% | 48 |
| 13 | 148 | 186 | 80% | 64 |
| 14 | 74 | 91 | 81% | 96 |
| 15 | 73 | 89 | 82% | 96 |
| 16 | 69 | 86 | 80% | 96 |

### Observations

1. **All-irreducible is the majority, and the fraction is stable at ~80%.**
   From n=6 onward the fraction of all-irreducible dissections holds steady
   at 79–84%. Being all-irreducible is the *generic* condition for random
   lattice dissections.

2. **The best all-irreducible count grows slowly.** The maximum all-irreducible
   tiling count rises from 4 (n=3) to 16 (n=7–9) to 48 (n=10–12) to 96 (n=14–16).
   This is far below the total maxima (336 at n=7, 800 at n=16), confirming that
   extreme flexibility requires fault lines.

3. **The n=13 champion (120 all-irreducible) is special for its count, not its
   irreducibility.** Having all tilings fault-free is common (80%); having 120 of
   them is extraordinary. Seed 99 produces a persistent champion that achieves
   48–96 all-irreducible tilings across n=10–16.

4. **Fault lines are a property of high-count dissections.** Dissections with many
   tilings are more likely to have *some* solutions that accidentally align along
   grid lines. Low-count dissections (1–16 tilings) are almost always all-irreducible.

5. **The Stomachion is NOT all-irreducible.** Only 32 of its 2,144 geometric tilings
   (1.5%) are fault-free. Its extraordinary flexibility comes at the cost of
   irreducibility — most of its tilings exploit optional fault lines (the midlines
   y=6 and x=6 each appear in 46% of solutions).
